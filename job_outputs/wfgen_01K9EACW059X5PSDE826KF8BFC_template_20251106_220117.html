12‑Section Birth Plan (Sample)

Print‑ready. Share with your care team on arrival. Keep flexible: clinical needs may change.

1) Environment & Communication

  • Quiet room, dim lights, limit nonessential interruptions
  • Introduce all staff on entry; share roles clearly
  • Preferences to be discussed with both parent and partner

2) Mobility & Monitoring

  • Encourage walking and position changes as tolerated
  • Intermittent fetal monitoring if medically appropriate
  • Wireless telemetry preferred if continuous monitoring required

3) Hydration & Nutrition

  • Clear fluids as allowed by hospital policy
  • Ice chips available; IV only if indicated

4) Pain Management

  • Non‑medicated coping first: shower, birthing ball, massage
  • Epidural on request; option to delay until active labor
  • Nitrous oxide if available

5) Induction & Augmentation

  • Discuss Bishop score; prefer membrane sweep before Pitocin if appropriate
  • Interventions explained with benefits/risks/alternatives

6) Second Stage (Pushing)

  • Open‑glottis pushing; positions of comfort
  • Perineal support, warm compresses to reduce tearing
  • Episiotomy only if medically necessary

7) Delivery Preferences

  • Partner to announce sex (if not previously known)
  • Golden hour skin‑to‑skin immediately if baby stable
  • Delayed cord clamping ~60–120 seconds when safe

8) Third Stage (Placenta)

  • Active management if indicated; otherwise physiologic
  • Placenta disposition per hospital policy

9) Newborn Care

  • Vitamin K and erythromycin: yes
  • First bath delayed >12 hours; measurements after bonding if possible
  • Room‑in; partner to accompany baby for procedures

10) Feeding Plan

  • Exclusive breastfeeding if possible; formula only by request or medical need
  • Lactation consult within first 12 hours

11) Visitors & Photos

  • Limit visitors to immediate family day 1
  • No social media posts without parent approval

12) Unplanned Cesarean

  • Clear drape or mirror if feasible; partner present
  • Skin‑to‑skin in OR/PACU when safe; gentle cesarean approach

Hospital‑Specific Tips (Sample for NYU Langone — Tisch)

  • Lactation Support
    Ask for lactation within the first morning after birth. Bring your feeding plan; formula is available upon request.
  • Meal Timing
    Cafeteria hours can be limited overnight. Pack shelf‑stable snacks for the partner.
  • Quiet Hours
    Use white noise to buffer night rounding; ask for a “Do Not Disturb” window if baby is sleeping and stable.

Insurance & Billing Insights

“Best” plans vary by state, employer options, and network. For NYC academic centers, families often report smooth maternity experiences with large PPO networks.

Coverage Types
  • PPO: widest hospital choice, higher premiums, lower referral friction
  • HMO/EPO: lower premiums, but stricter networks and referrals
Frequently Compatible in NYC
  • Blue Cross Blue Shield PPO (Empire BCBS)
  • UnitedHealthcare Choice/Choice Plus
  • Aetna PPO/Open Choice
Confirm your specific plan and provider IDs with the hospital’s insurance office for the most accurate, current network status.
Action Steps (Before 28 Weeks)
  • Verify in‑network status for OB, hospital, anesthesiology, neonatology
  • Ask for global maternity billing estimate and newborn coverage start
  • Preauthorize breast pump; confirm lactation visit coverage
Contact hospital financial counseling for up‑to‑date, plan‑specific guidance. Keep screenshots of verification calls.
